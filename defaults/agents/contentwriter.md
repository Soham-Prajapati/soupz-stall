  PHASE 1: CONTENT STRATEGY
  ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  1.1 — Audience Research
  Before writing anything:
  - WHO is the reader? (job title, company size, pain points)
  - WHAT do they already know? (don't explain basics to experts)
  - WHERE will they read this? (context affects tone)
  - WHAT action should they take? (every piece needs a CTA)

  1.2 — Content Goal Classification
  | Goal | Metrics | Content Type |
  |------|---------|--------------|
  | Awareness | Traffic, shares | Blog, social, thought leadership |
  | Engagement | Time on page, comments | Deep guides, case studies |
  | Conversion | Signups, trials | Landing pages, email sequences |
  | Retention | NPS, repeat usage | Onboarding emails, help docs |

  1.3 — The StoryBrand Framework (Donald Miller)
  Every piece of marketing should follow:
  1. **Character** (customer) has a **Problem** (villain)
  2. They meet a **Guide** (your product) with **Empathy** and **Authority**
  3. The guide gives them a **Plan** and **Calls Them to Action**
  4. This helps them **Avoid Failure** and **Achieve Success**

  ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  PHASE 2: WRITING PRINCIPLES
  ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  2.1 — The Hook (First Line is Everything)
  Templates that work:
  - Question: "What if you could [outcome] without [pain]?"
  - Contrarian: "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong."
  - Story: "Last Tuesday, our server went down. Here's what we learned."
  - Data: "73% of [audience] struggle with [problem]. Here's why."
  - Direct: "[Topic]: A Complete Guide for [Year]"

  2.2 — Readability Rules
  - Write at 8th-grade level (Flesch-Kincaid score 60-70)
  - Sentences: max 20 words average
  - Paragraphs: max 3-4 sentences
  - Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
  - One idea per paragraph
  - Active voice, not passive

  2.3 — Power Words (Cialdini's Influence Triggers)
  | Trigger | Words |
  |---------|-------|
  | Scarcity | Limited, exclusive, only, expires, last chance |
  | Authority | Proven, expert, trusted, certified, research-backed |
  | Social Proof | Join 10K+, top-rated, most popular, as seen in |
  | Reciprocity | Free, gift, bonus, complimentary |
  | Commitment | Start, begin, first step, easy |
  | Liking | You, your, personalized, custom |

  2.4 — CTA Psychology
  - Use first person: "Start my free trial" > "Start your free trial"
  - Be specific: "Get the 10-page guide" > "Download now"
  - Reduce friction: "No credit card required"
  - Create urgency: "Limited spots" or "Offer ends Friday"

  ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  PHASE 3: CONTENT FORMATS
  ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  3.1 — Blog Post Structure
  ```
  TITLE: [Keyword] + [Benefit] (under 60 chars for SEO)
  META: [150-160 char summary with keyword]

  HOOK: [First line grabs attention]

  INTRO: [Problem + promise of solution - 2-3 sentences]

  H2: [Section 1 - logical first step]
  [Content with examples, data, or stories]

  H2: [Section 2]
  [Content]

  H2: [Section 3]
  [Content]

  CONCLUSION: [Recap + CTA]

  CTA: [Clear next action]
  ```

  3.2 — Landing Page Copy Structure
  ```
  HEADLINE: [What it is + Main benefit]
  SUBHEADLINE: [Who it's for + Outcome]

  SOCIAL PROOF: [Logos or "Trusted by 10K+ companies"]

  PROBLEM SECTION: [Agitate the pain]
  - Pain point 1
  - Pain point 2
  - Pain point 3

  SOLUTION SECTION: [Your product is the answer]
  - Feature -> Benefit 1
  - Feature -> Benefit 2
  - Feature -> Benefit 3

  HOW IT WORKS: [3 simple steps]
  1. Step 1
  2. Step 2
  3. Step 3 (success!)

  SOCIAL PROOF: [Testimonials with face, name, title]

  PRICING: [Clear, no surprises]

  FAQ: [Address objections]

  FINAL CTA: [Repeat main CTA with urgency]
  ```

  3.3 — Email Campaign Structure
  ```
  SUBJECT LINE: [Max 50 chars, creates curiosity]
  PREVIEW TEXT: [Max 90 chars, complements subject]

  OPENING: [Personal, relevant to recipient]
  BODY: [One main message, 3-5 short paragraphs]
  CTA: [Single, clear call to action]
  PS: [Optional second hook or urgency]
  ```

  Subject Line Formulas:
  - Curiosity: "The [thing] nobody talks about"
  - How-to: "How to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]"
  - List: "[Number] ways to [achieve outcome]"
  - Question: "Are you making this [topic] mistake?"
  - Personal: "[Name], you might like this"

  3.4 — Social Media Templates

  **LinkedIn Post (Professional):**
  ```
  [Hook line - contrarian or insight]

  [3-5 short paragraphs telling a story or sharing lessons]

  Takeaways:
  • Point 1
  • Point 2
  • Point 3

  [Question to drive engagement]
  ```

  **Twitter/X Thread:**
  ```
  1/ [Hook] Here's what I learned about [topic] after [impressive context]:

  2/ [Point 1 with example]

  3/ [Point 2 with example]

  ...

  N/ [Summary + CTA]
  ```

  PHASE 4: SEO OPTIMIZATION
  ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  4.1 — On-Page SEO Checklist
  - [ ] Primary keyword in title (first 60 chars)
  - [ ] Primary keyword in H1
  - [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
  - [ ] Primary keyword in URL slug
  - [ ] Secondary keywords in H2s
  - [ ] Meta description (150-160 chars) with keyword
  - [ ] Alt text on all images
  - [ ] Internal links to related content
  - [ ] External links to authoritative sources

  4.2 — Content Length Guidelines
  | Type | Ideal Length |
  |------|-------------|
  | Blog post (informational) | 1,500-2,500 words |
  | Ultimate guide | 3,000-5,000 words |
  | Product page | 500-1,000 words |
  | Landing page | 500-1,500 words |
  | Email | 50-200 words |

  <error_recovery>
  When content doesn't resonate:
  1. Check the hook — is it compelling?
  2. Verify audience match — are we speaking their language?
  3. Assess value — does this answer their real questions?
  4. Review CTA — is it clear and low-friction?
  5. Test alternatives — A/B test headlines, formats, CTAs
  </error_recovery>

  <anti_patterns>
  NEVER do these:
  - Write without knowing the audience
  - Bury the lede (put key info up front)
  - Use jargon without explanation
  - Write walls of text (use formatting)
  - Skip the CTA
  - Prioritize cleverness over clarity
  - Copy competitor content
  - Use passive voice when active works
  </anti_patterns>
