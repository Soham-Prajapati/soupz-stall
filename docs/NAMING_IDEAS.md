# Name Alternatives for Soupz

> A hosted web IDE accessible from any device (phone, tablet, browser) that bridges to a local daemon on your machine. You code from anywhere using AI agents.

---

## The 15 Core Candidates

### 1. Relay
- **Reasoning:** Directly describes the core mechanic -- relaying prompts and responses between your browser and your local dev machine. A relay race passes the baton; Relay passes your intent to the machine that runs the code.
- **Spoken:** One word, two syllables, universally understood. "I'll Relay that to my machine." Works as both noun and verb.
- **Evokes:** Speed, handoff, teamwork, signal transmission, radio relays.
- **Domains:** relay.dev (taken by Facebook's GraphQL lib), getrelay.dev, userelay.dev, relay.sh, relay.run

### 2. Conduit
- **Reasoning:** An engineering-grade word for a channel or pipe that carries something from point A to point B. Your phone is a conduit to your dev machine.
- **Spoken:** Two syllables, KON-doo-it. Feels substantial and technical without being jargon.
- **Evokes:** Infrastructure, pipelines, electricity flowing through conduits, precision engineering.
- **Domains:** conduit.dev, getconduit.dev, conduit.sh, conduit.codes

### 3. Aether
- **Reasoning:** The classical "fifth element" -- the invisible medium that fills the space between things. Your code travels through the aether between your phone and your machine.
- **Spoken:** EE-ther. Elegant, slightly mythical. "Open Aether on your phone."
- **Evokes:** Invisible connectivity, something that just works without you seeing the wires, the space between stars, wireless transmission.
- **Domains:** aether.dev, aetherdev.com, useaether.dev, aether.sh

### 4. Span
- **Reasoning:** Double meaning -- a bridge spans a gap (phone to machine), and `<span>` is one of the most fundamental HTML elements. Short, punchy, dev-flavored.
- **Spoken:** One syllable. Snappy. "Just span into your project." Works as verb too.
- **Evokes:** Bridges, reach, HTML, covering distance, wingspan.
- **Domains:** span.dev, getspan.dev, span.sh, spancode.dev

### 5. Cove
- **Reasoning:** A sheltered harbor you can reach from the open sea. Your dev environment is the cove -- protected, local, yours -- and you sail in from any device, anywhere.
- **Spoken:** One syllable, warm and inviting. "Push that to Cove." Friendly, non-intimidating.
- **Evokes:** Safety, harbor, a place you return to, calm productivity, coastal retreat.
- **Domains:** cove.dev, getcove.dev, cove.sh, codeincove.dev

### 6. Vox
- **Reasoning:** Latin for "voice." Fits the voice-first coding angle (Sarvam STT, Kokoro TTS). You speak and agents listen. Also suggests giving voice to your ideas.
- **Spoken:** One syllable, rhymes with "box." Punchy. "Vox, build me a dashboard."
- **Evokes:** Voice command, communication, broadcasting, Latin authority, radio callsigns.
- **Domains:** vox.dev (likely taken -- Vox Media), voxcode.dev, usevox.dev, vox.sh

### 7. Tether
- **Reasoning:** Your phone is tethered to your dev machine -- not physically, but through the daemon connection. Like phone tethering but for development, not just internet.
- **Spoken:** Two syllables, familiar word. "Tether to your machine." Intuitive verb form.
- **Evokes:** Connection, mobile hotspot, keeping things linked, a lifeline.
- **Domains:** tether.dev, gettether.dev, tether.sh, tethercode.dev

### 8. Pulse
- **Reasoning:** The real-time heartbeat between your browser and your daemon. Every WebSocket ping, every Supabase Realtime event is a pulse confirming the link is alive.
- **Spoken:** One syllable, energetic. "Check your Pulse." Implies something living and responsive.
- **Evokes:** Heartbeat, vitality, real-time, rhythm, liveness checks, health monitoring.
- **Domains:** pulse.dev, getpulse.dev, pulsedev.com, pulse.sh, codepulse.dev

### 9. Drift
- **Reasoning:** You drift seamlessly between devices -- start on your laptop, continue on your phone, finish on a tablet. No friction, just flow.
- **Spoken:** One syllable, smooth. "Drift between your devices." Poetic without being pretentious.
- **Evokes:** Fluidity, effortlessness, snowdrift (layering), driftwood (natural movement), ocean currents.
- **Domains:** drift.dev, getdrift.dev (Drift chat tool exists), driftcode.dev, drift.sh

### 10. Pylon
- **Reasoning:** Pylons are the towers that carry power lines across distances. Your local machine is the power plant; Pylon carries that power to your phone or tablet.
- **Spoken:** Two syllables, PY-lon. Strong, structural. "Connect through Pylon."
- **Evokes:** Infrastructure, power transmission, towers, gateways, stability, Protoss pylons (gaming culture).
- **Domains:** pylon.dev, getpylon.dev, pylon.sh, pyloncode.dev

### 11. Nexus
- **Reasoning:** Latin for "connection" or "binding together." The central point where all your devices, agents, and code converge.
- **Spoken:** Two syllables, NEX-us. Feels like a flagship product name. "Your dev Nexus."
- **Evokes:** Hub, convergence, Google Nexus phones (familiar tech brand pattern), centrality.
- **Domains:** nexus.dev (likely taken), nexuscode.dev, getnexus.dev, devnexus.dev

### 12. Arca
- **Reasoning:** Latin for "chest" or "vault" -- your local machine as a treasure chest of compute. Also contains "arc," suggesting the trajectory of a signal arcing from device to machine.
- **Spoken:** Two syllables, AR-ka. Distinctive, memorable. "Deploy with Arca."
- **Evokes:** Treasure vault, ark (Noah's ark carrying everything), architecture, arc of connection.
- **Domains:** arca.dev, getarca.dev, arca.sh, arcacode.dev

### 13. Omni
- **Reasoning:** Latin prefix meaning "all" or "everywhere." Code from every device, with every agent, on every project. Universal access.
- **Spoken:** Two syllables, OM-nee. Bold, expansive. "Omni gives you access everywhere."
- **Evokes:** Omnipresence, omniscience, universal, comprehensive, God-mode for dev.
- **Domains:** omni.dev, omnidev.com, getomni.dev, omni.sh, omnicode.dev

### 14. Sync
- **Reasoning:** The core promise -- your devices are in sync. Your phone sees what your machine sees. Changes flow both ways, in real time.
- **Spoken:** One syllable, universally understood. "Sync to your machine." Already a verb in everyone's vocabulary.
- **Evokes:** Synchronization, harmony, matching state, real-time updates, clocks aligned.
- **Domains:** sync.dev (likely taken), devsync.dev, synccode.dev, getsync.dev, sync.sh

### 15. Loom
- **Reasoning:** A loom weaves threads together. Loom weaves the thread of your phone, the thread of your daemon, the thread of your AI agents into a single fabric of productivity.
- **Spoken:** One syllable, rich texture. "Weave it together with Loom." (Loom video tool exists, but different space.)
- **Evokes:** Weaving, craftsmanship, threads (computing threads too), textile artistry, interconnection.
- **Domains:** loom.dev (Loom video exists), devloom.dev, getloom.dev, loom.sh, codeloom.dev

- **Domains:** setu.dev, getsetu.dev, setu.sh, setucode.dev

### 19. Vyom (Sanskrit)

### 22. Floe

## 5 New Architecture & Orchestration Names

### 31. Prism
- **Reasoning:** A prism takes a single beam of light and breaks it into multiple parallel spectrums. Just like Soupz takes a single complex prompt and breaks it into parallel agent workstreams (BMAD).
- **Spoken:** One syllable, PRIZ-um. Sharp and memorable.
- **Evokes:** Light, clarity, parallel streams, refraction, Pink Floyd.
- **Domains:** prism.dev (likely taken), prismcode.dev, useprism.dev

### 32. Maestro
- **Reasoning:** The conductor of an orchestra. Perfectly captures the "Master Orchestrator" and "Team of Teams" philosophy. It directs the specialized agents without playing the instruments itself.
- **Spoken:** Three syllables, MY-stro. Cultured and authoritative.
- **Evokes:** Conducting, harmony, leadership, classical music, mastery.
- **Domains:** maestro.dev, getmaestro.dev, devmaestro.com

### 33. Synapse
- **Reasoning:** The connection point where signals are passed between neurons. Your laptop is the brain, your phone is the synapse. The parallel agents are the neural network.
- **Spoken:** Two syllables, SIN-aps. Scientific and fast.
- **Evokes:** Brainpower, neural networks, instant transmission, AI.
- **Domains:** synapse.dev, getsynapse.dev, synapsecodes.com

### 34. Kinaara (Sanskrit/Hindi)
- **Reasoning:** Meaning "edge" or "shore." You are coding on the edge (your phone), looking out at the ocean (your dev machine). A bridge connects two shores.
- **Spoken:** Three syllables, kih-NAH-ra. Musical and unique.
- **Evokes:** Coastlines, edge computing, boundaries, Indian heritage.
- **Domains:** kinaara.dev, getkinaara.dev, kinaaracode.dev

### 35. Hub
- **Reasoning:** The absolute center of a network. Simple, unpretentious, universally understood. Your laptop is the Hub, all other devices and agents connect to it.
- **Spoken:** One syllable. Blunt and effective.
- **Evokes:** Centrality, networks, spokes, GitHub, concentration of power.
- **Domains:** hub.dev (definitely taken), codehub.dev, devhub.sh
